# AnEnemy — player's manual

*Two clone colonies of sea anemones meet on a tidal rock. Where they touch,
neither will yield. Engorge your venom tentacles, lash across the no-man's-land,
and sting the rival clone until it deflates and cedes the rock.*

## The premise

Aggregating anemones (*Anthopleura elegantissima*) live in large, genetically
identical clones. Where two clones meet they hold a **no-man's-land** boundary
and fight in ranks using **acrorhagi** — specialised tentacles near the base,
packed with high-density venom used *only* against rival anemones (their
ordinary tentacles just catch prey). A strike leaves patches of stinging cells
on the enemy's soft body. Battles run from minutes to days; the loser deflates,
withdraws, and relocates. You command the front-line warrior polyps.

## Controls

- **Crank forward ⟳** — engorge your acrorhagi (raise charge → reach + sting).
- **Crank back ⟲** — deflate the charge (save energy, bail out of a wind-up).
- **Ⓐ** — release / lash across the gap with your current charge. Also confirms
  on the title, map, spoils, and result screens.
- **✛ right / left** — lean **in** on the rival or **back** off (see *Positioning*).
- **Ⓑ** — **brace**: hunker down for a moment to deflect the gull's peck.

*(If the crank is docked, an on-screen prompt reminds you it's the main control.)*

## How to play

**The core gamble is feed vs. fight.** Engorging your acrorhagi *stops* your
feeding tentacles and *burns* energy — the bigger the charge, the more it costs
and the longer you stand exposed. Over-attack and you starve mid-lash;
over-feed and the rival pushes the boundary into you.

- **Reach is the skill gate.** A lash only connects if its reach spans the gap.
  Reach grows with charge, so a limp, under-charged strike whiffs harmlessly.
  The **connect tick** on the charge meter marks the minimum charge that reaches
  — charge past it to sting *harder*, but you bleed more energy and stay open
  longer.
- **Timing beats spamming.** Getting stung while you are charging **drops your
  charge**, so a well-timed strike into the rival's recovery window is worth
  more than a flurry.
- **Damage is sting, not health.** Each landed lash adds to the rival's **sting
  total**, drawn as stipple **scars** accreting on its oral disc. When the total
  crosses that anemone's **tolerance**, it deflates, withdraws, and the duel is
  yours. (You lose the same way.)
- **Feed to refuel.** Between wind-ups, let your feeding tentacles catch the
  drifting plankton — that regenerates your energy. But **leaning in stops your
  feeding**, so an aggressive posture has a hidden cost.

## Positioning

The gap between the two clones is not fixed. It **opens wider or narrower each
skirmish** (whoever holds more of the rock presses their front line forward),
and the **connect tick moves with it** — so read the tick fresh every fight.

Within a duel you steer the gap yourself:

- **✛ right — advance.** Creep toward the rival. The gap shrinks, so a *lower*
  charge now reaches — but you slide into the rival's reach too, it **burns
  energy**, and it **stops you feeding**. A committing, aggressive stance.
- **✛ left — retreat.** Back off. The gap opens, your lashes need more charge to
  land, but you're safer and free to feed. A patient, defensive stance.

If neither clone can end it, a **sudden-death** clock eventually erodes both
tolerances until someone deflates — no more endless stalemates.

## The tide

Tide is the master clock, shown on the gauge.

- **High tide** — you are submerged: feed and fight normally.
- **Low tide** — the water recedes and the rock is bared. Both anemones
  **contract** into balls: you can't lash, but contracting **heals** accumulated
  sting. The catch is **desiccation** — exposed to air, you slowly bleed energy
  until the water returns. Low tide is also when the predators come.

## Hazards at low tide

- **Gull** — dives from above and **pecks** the rock, adding a jolt of sting to
  whichever anemone it hits. It alternates which side it stoops on. Watch its
  telegraph and **tap Ⓑ to brace** as it strikes — a well-timed brace deflects
  the peck entirely (a shell cap flashes over you).
- **Aeolidia nudibranch** — a sea slug that crawls along the bared rock toward
  the richer, fatter clone and **grazes** it, draining energy as it feeds.

Neither predator takes sides — they are pure environment and will happily maul
you *or* the rival.

## The territory war

A single duel is one skirmish. The rock is a **row of cells** with a boundary
between your clone and the rival's.

- Win a skirmish → the boundary shifts one cell your way; lose → it shifts
  toward you.
- **Held territory reinforces you.** The more net cells you hold, the more
  start-of-duel energy you carry into the next skirmish (and the rival carries
  the inverse) — so a lead **snowballs**. Push the boundary until you **clear
  the rock**, and the war is won. Lose all your cells and the clone is routed.

## The campaign — rival strains

Clear a rock and a new rival clone claims the next one. Each **strain** fights
with its own personality — not just different numbers, but a **different brain**
(told apart by its dither pattern):

- **Actinia — the glass cannon.** Over-commits: charges fast, presses in, and
  pounces the instant you're open — even while you're feeding. Low tolerance,
  vicious sting. Punish its recovery windows before it punishes yours.
- **Metridium — the economy tank.** Turtles: banks a huge energy buffer,
  **retreats to feed**, and only lashes on a clean opening. Endures a vast sting
  total. Starve it of openings and out-charge it.
- **Urticina — the attrition wall.** Holds the midline, patient, and partly
  **resistant** to your venom so raw damage bleeds off. Win on clean connects.
- **The Aggressor — the boss.** Presses greedily from behind heavy resistance.
  Hold your charge, pick your moment, and don't get baited into trading.

### Spoils

Clear a rock and you **choose one boon** — *thicker body* (more tolerance),
*sharper venom* (harder lashes), or *wider gape* (faster feeding). Boons stack
for the rest of that campaign, so shape your clone toward the fights ahead.

Win a whole campaign and your record persists between sessions (best strains
cleared, campaigns won).

## Tips

1. **Learn the connect tick.** Everything below it is a wasted, energy-burning
   whiff. Your cheapest reliable hit is charge *just past* the tick.
2. **Strike into recovery.** After the rival lashes it retracts and is
   vulnerable — that is the moment to land a big one.
3. **Bank a buffer before you commit.** Don't start a full wind-up on empty
   energy; feed to a comfortable margin, then charge through the drain in one
   go rather than stalling at the threshold.
4. **Low tide is a reset.** Use the contraction to heal off scars — but keep an
   eye on your energy as desiccation and the slug both bleed it.
5. **Territory compounds.** An early skirmish win pays for itself in
   reinforcement energy; press a lead hard before the rival can claw the
   boundary back.
6. **Against tanks, don't trade — out-time.** Metridium and Urticina win the
   attrition fight; beat them on clean, well-timed connects, not volume.
